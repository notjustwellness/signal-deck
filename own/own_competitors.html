OWN.

The field.

Four rivals, and our wedge. Where we're the same, where we're not, and where to optimize.

The lead is uncomfortable: our honesty is real, but it is not, by itself, a weapon that wins customers. Every one of these four beats us on something we don't yet have — distribution, a warm audience, or claim latitude. So "where do we optimize" is less about being more honest and more about acquiring their reach while pointing our honesty at their specific liabilities.

Where we're similar — and why that's the danger

Metabolic/glucose positioning overlaps Supergut and Glucose Goddess — the "flatten the curve" territory is contested, not open. PHGG fiber — Supergut uses the same Sunfiber PHGG we plan to, confirming the product layer is not a moat. Premium price + sachet format — Supergut is ~$30 in stick packs; we're not novel there either.

The takeaway: our positioning is copyable and our ingredient is commodity. If differentiation lives in words on a landing page, we lose. It has to live in proof + execution.

Where we genuinely differ — the wall
  • Honesty as posture. All four overclaim (ColonBroom, GG), under-dose the claim (Olipop), or borrow the proof (Supergut). We're the only one that refuses — "a sign, not a promise." In a field walking into lawsuits and FTC actions, that refusal is bulletproof.
  • Dose. ≥10g vs their 3.6–9g — a real, provable, headline-able edge.
  • Zero-texture pour vs Metamucil's jelly reputation.
  • Anti-subscription-trap (pause-beats-cancel, one tap) — the literal inverse of ColonBroom's cancel-maze.
The opening the whole field shares
Nobody has proof on the exact product a customer buys. Our CGM study on the actual product, in healthy optimizers, leapfrogs all four at once.
Supergut's RCT is on shakes in a dysglycemic population; Glucose Goddess has no product trial; ColonBroom and Olipop borrow ingredient-level science at doses their product doesn't deliver. This is not one differentiator among many — it's the wedge. It should be the centerpiece, sequenced before any loud launch.
Where to optimize — ranked
  • Make the healthy-population, own-product study the hero. The only thing none of them can answer. Everything else is supporting cast.
  • Weaponize the dose gap. Named-to-gram, ≥10g, beside their 3.6–6g. "The others show you a graph; we show you the grams."
  • Weaponize texture vs Metamucil and easy-cancel vs ColonBroom — cheap, concrete, ownable contrasts.
  • Absorb the Glucose Goddess audience — don't fight it. She built a massive "glucose curve" audience on borrowed evidence. Be the honest version they graduate to; her followers are our beachhead.
  • Solve our actual gap: reach. Honesty makes borrowed reach convert and stick; it doesn't generate reach. The affordable substitute is creator-seeding (many small credible voices showing their own real curve), then retail after proof. Budget for reach — don't assume the honesty sells itself.
  • Never board the GLP-1 train. Supergut's Ozempic tether is its vulnerability. GLP-1 is a demand door, never a hype claim.
The one place we're outgunned — you should hear it

Metamucil owns psyllium's FDA-authorized cholesterol / heart-disease claim (7g/day soluble fiber). (Likely) our PHGG can't match that regulatory high ground — psyllium's lipid evidence is stronger than guar's. If cardiovascular ever becomes a pillar, a "geriatric" brand out-proofs us on that specific claim. Our answer isn't to chase the cholesterol claim — it's to win on the glucose curve + felt benefit + honesty, where they can't credibly follow. Don't pick the fight where the legacy brand holds the FDA card.
