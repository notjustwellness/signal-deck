THE IDEA (so every choice makes sense)
--------------------------------------
Some creatures send a flash of living light into the dark — a SIGNAL. We
see the flash; we never see what it cost: cellular energy, poured out in
an instant. This film lives in the DARK (where the energy gathers), lets
brief flashes reveal the world for a heartbeat, and ONCE goes inside a
flash to show the cellular machinery behind it. The brand is the one
signal that doesn't go dark. Tagline lands literally: "send the right
signal." Weight everything to the dark and the cost — NOT the pretty
flash (that's the trap).

HOW TO USE THIS FILE
--------------------
AI generates two kinds of clips: (a) bioluminescent FLASHES in darkness,
and (b) the INTERIOR cellular machinery (the cost). The film lives in the
editor: the black, the flash timing, the charge-up builds, the sound. In
post: assemble against long black, build a glow before each flash, go
inside once, lay the slow-entrainment bed, end on the brand flash that
holds. Wordmark + sound are SUPPLIED FILES — never AI-generated.

THE RULES (do not break)
------------------------
- The light is the CREATURE'S SIGNAL, never a human benefit. No human
  glowing. "glow = your vitality" is a claim — don't.
- The product never causes a flash. No outcome shown.
- Don't worship the flash. The dark and the cost are the subject.


================================================================
GLOBAL STYLE BIBLE  —  prepend on EVERY shot
================================================================
Two looks, one palette. Photoreal. EXTERIOR flashes: bioluminescence in
near-total darkness — light brief and soft, revealing surroundings for a
heartbeat, deep black all around. INTERIOR (the cost): extreme cellular
close-up, machinery pouring a warm OXBLOOD-RED glow (#AD4236) into the
reaction. Across both: deep espresso-black, bone highlights, the single
oxblood-amber glow as the through-line. Low-key, volumetric, lots of
negative space, slow and reverent. The darkness is the subject; the light
is rare.

GLOBAL NEGATIVE PROMPT  —  append on every shot
-----------------------------------------------
text, captions, watermark, logo, brand name, bright or daytime scenes,
full/even lighting, human glowing, the creature's whole body in clear
view, fast cutting, distorted anatomy, cartoon, plastic CGI look,
oversaturated, garish neon, shaky cam

THE SHOTS  (each is one generation; assemble against BLACK)
================================================================

--- SHOT 1 · FIREFLY FLASH (the first signal) · ~4s ---
Total darkness. A single firefly ignites — a soft warm flash that, for one
heartbeat, reveals a dense dark forest around it (leaves, branches, mist)
— then the light fades and everything returns to black. Photoreal, deep
shadow, the light gentle not harsh. Slow.

--- SHOT 2 · THE CHARGE (the cost, before the light) · ~6s ---
Near-total black. A faint warm amber-red glow slowly gathers and builds in
one spot — energy pooling, intensifying, straining, as if charging — but
it does NOT fully flash; hold it on the verge. Macro, abstract, the glow
breathing inside darkness. Very slow build.

--- SHOT 3 · PLANKTON WAVE (the release) · ~4s ---
A dark night beach. A wave breaks and the disturbed water blooms with blue
bioluminescent plankton light along the surf for a moment, then fades to
black. Photoreal, deep night, the blue glow soft and electric. Slow motion.

--- SHOT 4 · INSIDE THE FLASH (the cost, revealed) · ~6s ---
Extreme microscopic close-up: we are INSIDE a light organ at the cellular
scale — machinery and mitochondria pouring a warm oxblood-red/amber glow
into a luminescent chemical reaction, furious activity, energy streaming
along channels. The hidden cost behind a single flash. Slow, dense, alive.

--- SHOT 5 · DEEP-SEA GLOW (alone in the black) · ~4s ---
The pitch-black deep ocean. A lone deep-sea creature emits a soft glow
that reveals only itself, drifting in infinite darkness, then dims. No
surroundings, no surface. Photoreal, cold, lonely, beautiful. Very slow.

--- SHOT 6 · THE TURN (human interior) · ~5s ---
Extreme cellular close-up of HUMAN interior tissue — capillaries, the same
warm oxblood-red glow and machinery, IDENTICAL in look to Shot 4. A subtle
human pulse. (It must be indistinguishable from the firefly interior.) No
face, no body, no glowing skin — only the cellular interior. Slow.

NOT GENERATED — built in the editor

--- THE AFTERCOST ---
After a flash, hold on BLACK (the creature spent, recharging). Long dark
beats between flashes are part of the film, not dead air.

--- THE LINE (0:52) ---
On black, no voice: "You see the signal. Not what it costs to send it."
Then smaller: "You don't start it. You feed it."

--- THE BRAND FLASH (0:57) ---
On black, the wordmark (signal_wordmark_on_dark.png) rises like a flash —
but unlike every creature's, it does NOT go dark. Two offset copies drift
into one as signal_meditation.wav reaches unison; the glow holds steady.
Line beneath: send the right signal. Slow fade.

ASSEMBLY (in your editor)
================================================================
TIMING (60s): 0:00 firefly flash · 0:08 the charge (dark) · 0:18 plankton
flash · 0:24 inside-the-cost · 0:36 aftercost + deep-sea · 0:44 the turn
(human interior) · 0:52 the line · 0:57 brand flash that holds.

THE DARK IS THE FILM: most of the runtime is near-black. Flashes are
seconds. Let the black breathe — the disproportion of dark-to-light is the
argument. Don't fill the silence with light.

THE CHARGE: ramp a building glow before flashes so the energy is felt, not
just popped. THE COST: dissolve through one flash into the cellular machine
(Shot 4), graded to match the human interior (Shot 6) so the turn lands
with no announced cut.

AUDIO: signal_meditation.wav (~60s) under the whole film — two tones drift
to unison, steady ~60 bpm heartbeat; soft accents on each flash; the tones
settle to unison only at the brand flash. Final mix to -14 LUFS.

CUTDOWNS: :15 / :06 reuse the spine — :06 = one flash + inside-the-cost +
the brand flash. 9:16 vertical for paid/organic.
