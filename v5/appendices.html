A1 — The Platform Thesis

Energy is the foundation. Every signal supports it.

signal's launch SKU is positioned around skin because skin is the most visible expression of cellular energy decline. But the underlying biology that makes signal work is not specific to skin — it's specific to cellular energy. That's the thesis that lets a single brand architecture support eight discrete SKUs without becoming incoherent, and it's why v5 leads with energy rather than with hydration.

The science we're building on

Volkmar Weissig (President, World Mitochondria Society) published a 2025 review in the Journal of Mitochondria, Plastids and Endosymbiosis titled "Mitochondrial dysfunction as the 'mother' of all hallmarks of aging." His core argument: of the twelve canonical hallmarks of aging (López-Otín et al. 2023), mitochondrial dysfunction sits upstream of all the others. Mitochondria don't just produce ATP — they also run intercellular communication via mtDAMPs, mitochondrial-derived peptides, cardiolipin, and N-formyl peptides. When the energy economy fails, every other process that maintains tissue (DNA repair, proteostasis, autophagy, telomere maintenance, stem cell function) starts underperforming because it can't run without ATP.

The crystallizing line, from the consumer-facing thread that surfaced this thesis: "It's not one hallmark failing. It's the energy behind all of them."

How this maps to signal's 8-SKU platform

If cellular energy is the foundation, then every SKU on the INSDR platform is a different expression of the same biology — nourishing mitochondria in the cell type that matters for the use case the buyer is reaching for that day.

Why the launch SKU is signal (glow)

Skin gives the buyer something visible to validate against. Every chew is supporting the cellular machinery that holds water in skin tissue, and that machinery runs on energy. That's why astaxanthin (4mg/day) is the energy keystone of the formulation — it's one of the most-studied protectors of mitochondrial membranes, the structures dermal fibroblasts use to make ATP. HA and phytoceramides supply the substrates the cells use; astaxanthin protects the energy plant that uses them. The launch SKU is the most defensible entry because the visible outcome (skin) is the part of the body where energy decline shows up first to the mirror.

Why this is the platform's moat

Every future SKU emerges from the same biological foundation, so brand expansion doesn't require a new story — it requires a new application of an already-established one. This is the structural reason signal can scale to eight SKUs without diluting brand meaning. The brand verb — "send the right signal" — is also the literal biology. Mitochondria are signaling hubs. The tagline is the mechanism.

Important: claim discipline

signal does not claim to "treat mitochondrial dysfunction" or "reverse cellular aging." Those are pharmaceutical claims that signal as a dietary supplement cannot make. What signal claims is that its formulation supports the systems that are studied as part of healthy cellular function. The platform thesis above describes the biological framework we are building toward. The launch SKU's specific outcome claim is skin hydration, measured in the pilot study via corneometry and TEWL — not mitochondrial endpoints.

A2 — Try This

Energy gives you capacity. Presence tells you where to spend it.

A1 is about cellular energy — the biological fuel. This section is about what you do with it. Energy without presence is wasted; presence without energy is exhausting. Together, they produce what might be the closest thing to feeling invincible the body is built to feel. signal is designed to deliver both in the same 90 seconds: the chew provides the substrates and the ritual provides the reset.

Before you read another word about signal, do this. It takes 90 seconds and requires nothing but your jaw and your attention. This is what the format does — minus the active ingredients.

The Exercise

chew nothing. focus on everything.

  1. Set a 90-second timer.
  2. Close your mouth and start chewing as if you had a piece of gum.
  3. Pay attention to your jaw. The muscles working. Your tongue. Your breath through your nose.
  4. Notice that you can't scroll. You can't text. You can't be anywhere else.
  5. When the timer ends, notice what just happened.

What you just experienced

You took a 90-second pause — without an app, without a guide, without trying. The format forced it. That's the presence half of the product. Now pair it with the energy half (A1): every chew also delivers astaxanthin, HA, phytoceramides, and cofactor support for the mitochondrial machinery that powers every cell doing work during those 90 seconds and the hours that follow. The ritual gives you presence. The ingredients give you energy. Together, they're the closest thing to empowerment signal can credibly deliver.

This is positioned as retention proof, not as the lead. The reason people buy signal is the visible outcome (skin, and later the use-case-specific outcomes of each SKU). The reason they keep signal is the 90 seconds — the pairing of energy and presence that turns the routine into a ritual they actually want to do.

A3 — The Sensory Science

The pause has a clinical name.

If A1 is the biology and A2 is the practice, this is the clinical framework underneath both. The 5-4-3-2-1 sensory grounding technique is a CBT-derived intervention for acute anxiety, panic, and dissociation. It works by forcing attention to immediate sensory input — breaking the rumination loop. The 90-second signal ritual engages the same sensory channels through taste, texture, jaw proprioception, breath, and sound. That's why the practice in A2 works without the product: the sensory pattern alone is enough to produce the presence effect. The product makes it daily, repeatable, and paired with the energy substrates that give the resulting state something to spend itself on.

The Technique

name what your senses are doing.

Standard 5-4-3-2-1 protocol: 5 things you see, 4 things you feel, 3 things you hear, 2 things you smell, 1 thing you taste. The act of cataloguing pulls attention out of internal narrative and into physical present.

A7 — IP & Patent Strategy

Patents are the fence. The brand is the moat.

Let's be honest about how IP works in ingestible formulations: it doesn't, not by itself. Utility patents on supplement formulas are notoriously hard to enforce. Prior art is vast — hyaluronic acid, phytoceramides, and astaxanthin are all available today as individual supplements from dozens of brands. A patent claim on "the combination of X + Y + Z in a chew" is a defensive filing, not a competitive moat, and any investor who has lived through a CPG category will tell you the same thing.

We file anyway — because priority dates, assignment clauses, and design coverage are cheap insurance against a copycat lawsuit. But we don't pitch the patent stack to investors as the reason signal wins. The real defensibility is the thing that took Grüns to a $1.2B exit in April 2026: daily-use habit metrics, review velocity, subscription retention, and retail distribution. Grüns didn't win on IP. They won on 80% daily-use rates, 85K+ verified reviews, and a shelf position Unilever couldn't easily replicate. That's the playbook.

The defensive filing plan

Layer 1

provisional utility patent

$3K–$5K. Filed before any public demo. Locks a 12-month priority date on the format (dissolvable chew + liquid center + packet architecture). Defensive, not offensive. Cheap insurance against a fast-following copycat.

Layer 2

design patent

$2K–$3K. Covers the visual identity of the packet + container + the chew form factor itself. Faster to issue, harder to design around, and meaningfully stronger than utility filings in this category.

Layer 3

trade secrets

The exact compression profile, flavor encapsulation ratio, and lipid carrier formula that give the chew its progressive dissolve, time the liquid-center flavor burst at ~10 seconds, and keep the fat-soluble actives stable inside the center. Confection-industry technique, not a proprietary absorption system. CMO under NDA. Never disclosed.

Layer 4

what we don't patent

HA. Phytoceramides. Astaxanthin. These are prior art — decades of research, dozens of commercial sources. Patenting them would be a waste of money and would invite invalidation challenges on day one.

What the moat actually is

The seven-layer moat stack in the main deck (slide 9) does not list IP. That's deliberate. The things that actually make signal hard to copy are: (1) the sensory ritual that creates daily habit, (2) the cellular energy thesis that no other beauty-from-within brand has claimed, (3) the clinical doses that competitors would have to reformulate and reprice to match, (4) first-mover partnerships, (5) the celebrity cap table and Asia distribution via Vanness Wu, and (6) the daily-use habit metrics we earn in the first 6 months of launch. Patents are #0 on that list — table stakes, not strategy.

Filing sequence

M1–M2: Provisional utility filed. CMO MSA includes assignment-of-inventions clause.
M2–M3: Design patent filed once final packet/container design is locked.
M11: Convert provisional to non-provisional before 12-month deadline — only if commercial traction justifies the $15K–$25K full utility filing cost.
M14–M18: File international (PCT) for Asia distribution coverage — only after product-market fit is demonstrated.

A8 — Pilot Study Protocol

How we move Efficacy to PASS.

The Efficacy gate moves from PASS-path to PASS when we have direct evidence that signal as a combined formulation produces measurable skin hydration improvement. The individual ingredients are already proven. The combination is what we need to test. Critical: the pilot measures skin hydration endpoints, not mitochondrial function. The energy framing in the main deck is a platform thesis, not a clinical claim about the launch SKU.

Why this is the right study

Corneometry and TEWL are the two gold-standard non-invasive measures of skin hydration. Both are used in the published RCTs we already cite (Oe 2017, Bizot 2017). Replicating the methodology means our pilot results plug directly into the existing literature — no new measurement framework, no methodological debate. Mitochondrial endpoints (mtDNA copy number, OCR, membrane potential) are deliberately NOT used in this pilot; those belong to pharmaceutical-grade studies, not dietary supplement PoCs.

A9 — Send a Signal

The brand name is the launch mechanic.

Most viral gifting campaigns are bolt-ons — a referral code, a generic discount, a mechanic that has nothing to do with the product. signal is different. The product literally delivers a signal, so the act of sending one to a friend is on-brand by definition. The CTA is the brand name. Under development: extend from launch campaign to permanent embedded feature (subscribers retain ongoing send ability to first-time recipients).

The Mechanic

send one. get one free.

Pay $5 to send a 4-chew packet to a friend. We ship one to them and one to you — two people, two packets, one ritual. The brand spreads at near-breakeven acquisition cost vs. the $25–$50 typical CAC for DTC supplements.

Why this works specifically for signal

  • Brand-name alignment: "send a signal" is what the product is. The campaign IS the brand promise.
  • Low-friction sample: 4 chews is enough to experience the format, the flavor, and the 90-second ritual. It's a real product trial, not a teaser.
  • Reciprocity loop: The sender feels generous (they paid). The recipient feels chosen (someone thought of them). Both have skin in the game.
  • Built-in story: "My friend sent me this thing called signal" is a better intro than any ad we could buy.
  • Asia activation: Vanness's audience can send signals across borders — the campaign becomes the entry vehicle for international expansion at zero incremental brand spend.

A10 — Sensory & Packaging

Designed for tear, crunch, and texture.

Two sensory moments are real and provable: the packet tear and the first-bite crunch. We do not claim a "sonic logo," "Pavlovian cue," or any liquid slosh effect — those were ruled out as overclaims. What we DO claim is engineered into the materials. Under the energy thesis, the felt-feedback of the ritual is the consumer-facing evidence that something is happening — the presence-based sensory receipt that every other beauty supplement fails to deliver.

The Tear

laser-scored V-notch.

Material: Soft-touch OPP front / aluminum foil mid-layer / clear PET back. Notch: Laser-scored V on the top edge for clean tear without scissors. Cost: $0.08–$0.12 per packet at MOQ.

The Crunch

xylitol shell hardness.

The first bite resistance comes from a compressed xylitol shell. Tunable hardness in the chew matrix spec. Same compression principle as Hi-Chew — hard initial bite, progressive dissolve over 90 seconds.

The Packet

matte front. clear back.

Front: matte Signal-red with "send the right" overline + "signal" wordmark. Back: clear PET window showing the chew inside — you can see the product before you tear. 4 chews per packet. 1 packet = 1 serving.

The Container

60 packets per box.

Deep Evening (#1A1410) primary container. 60 packets per box = 60-day supply at 1 serving/day. Recyclable paperboard with foil-stamped wordmark. Premium unboxing without plastic clamshell.

A11 — Flavor Profiles

Cooling, hydrating-coded, refreshing. The "summer" SKU. Cucumber + mint give it a spa cue without entering wellness-cliché territory. Watermelon carries the sweetness.
