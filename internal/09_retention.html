Internal · Retention & Adherence

The one you keep taking

How we make it the one you keep taking — without faking a feeling.

Adherence is the spine of the whole thesis: "the supplement you'll actually keep taking." But the product can't be felt — so we can't lean on a buzz to bring people back. That instinct shaped this doc — yet the external churn data (Ch 02) says the felt gap is only the fourth-largest reason people quit. The bigger ones are the subscription and the price, and we defend those first. Throughout, we refuse the easy fixes (a fake sensation, a fabricated dashboard, a guilt-trip).

The one thing to take away

Churn has four drivers, and we'd been building around the smallest. In order: feeling trapped by the subscription (~30%), the price question against a cheaper tub (~25%), forgetting until bottles pile up (~15–20%), and "I didn't feel anything" (~15%). Our answers, in that order: an honest on/off switch, proof that justifies the premium, the ritual + dose-mirror, and the three honest felt levers. The felt work is real — it's just one front, not the whole game.

We compete on adherence through effortlessness, not accountability. This ladders to the Foundation Brief; if anything here contradicts it, the brief wins.

Chapter 01

The thesis — adherence is the spine

Cellular energy is the headline; adherence is the spine underneath it.

A foundation product only works if you keep taking it.

  • "The supplement you'll actually keep taking." The meta-benefit. Most supplements fail not because the formula is wrong but because the bottle ends up in the cabinet. We compete on whether the vessel ends up empty.
  • Effortlessness, not accountability. The one competitor in this lane (CORE7) drives adherence through accountability — coaching, nagging, streaks. We drive it through effortlessness: the easiest daily thing you'll do. Minimalism is the strategy, not a constraint.
  • Re-order is the metric. Because there's no felt mirror, re-order / retention is the truest signal we have that the product earns its place. It's the LTV metric (doc 07) and the real-world effect proof (doc 05, signal #7).
Chapter 02

What actually drives churn

Name the real drivers plainly — including the uncomfortable one: we'd been building around the smallest.

A stimulant brand brings you back with a buzz. A weight-loss brand brings you back with the scale. We have neither — a cellular-foundation product works below the threshold of sensation. That's the weakness we feel most acutely, so we instinctively built this whole doc around it. The external data says we over-indexed.

The founding cohort is the live experiment

For an imperceptible product, a re-order miss is as likely a conviction/mechanic problem as a product one. So the founding cohort is instrumented to tell those apart: when someone lapses, we learn why — subscription friction? price? "forgot / didn't see the point"? product? — before we scale spend. The four-front table above is our borrowed prior; the cohort is how we replace it with our own numbers. Fixing the wrong thing is the trap; the cohort exists to stop us doing it.

Chapter 04

The dose-mirror — the keystone mechanic

Its first job is to fight forgetting (the #3 churn driver) — a reason to re-open, re-engage, stay in the habit. Its second is to be something real to feel about the product without faking what they feel from it.

The grammar — two things only, never a third

Every touch narrates exactly two things: (1) the dose the customer has actually delivered to their body, and (2) what the published science says that dose supports. It never asserts what is happening inside them — we can't verify that, and a name-the-dose brand won't pretend to. The forbidden third thing is the fabricated read-out ("your energy is up 18%").

Email, not an app. The dose-mirror lives in email. An app with streaks and check-ins is the accountability lane (CORE7's game) — it pulls against effortlessness and invites the guilt-trip we refuse. Email narrates; it doesn't nag.

Chapter 05

The ritual & the vessel

The part of retention we fully control. Make the daily act easy, visible, and honest to leave.
  • Visibility = adherence. The counter-kept vessel is designed to look good and stay in sight — an empty vessel means a finished month (doc 02). The product that's seen is the product that's taken.
  • The daily moment. Same time, every day — the bundled creatine fuses into the flagship's moment so the ritual is one act, not two. "One signal a day. It adds up."
  • The honest on/off switch — our answer to the #1 churn driver. Feeling trapped by the subscription is the single biggest reason people quit the category (Ch 02). So pausing or stopping is one tap — no retention maze (doc 07). Honest retention compounds; trapped retention rots. We'd rather see a real switch-off and fix the product than bury it under friction — and the brands that bury it are the ones bleeding ~30% of cancels to resentment.
Chapter 06

What we refuse

The retention surface is the single highest claim-risk part of the brand. These are bright lines, not preferences.
  • No fabricated efficacy dashboard. Under launch pressure someone will propose "your cellular score went up 18%." That one feature detonates the brand. Any read-out reflects consistency + the customer's own real data — never a Signal-generated result.
  • No manufactured sensation. We never bolt on a stimulant to make the product "feel" like it works (paraxanthine / NMN-as-mirror rejected, doc 02).
  • No placebo-fishing self-reports. We don't ask leading questions ("feeling more energy?") designed to manufacture a yes.
  • No health-fear loss-framing. Retention copy is maintenance and identity ("this is who you are now"), never "stop and you'll deteriorate" — that's off-brand and a disease-adjacent claim.
  • Biomarker / wearable framing is the highest-risk idea we hold. If we ever surface a real read-out, it routes through Zone Halo reg review and stays observation-only, never implied causation — we show what was observed, we never claim Signal caused it.
Chapter 07

Measuring it

One metric leads; the cohort tells us what's behind it.
  • Re-order rate is the headline metric — the truest read on whether the product earns its place, and the input to LTV (doc 07).
  • Instrument the cohort, not just the number. A sub-target re-order rate is a question, not an answer: lapsed re-orderers are asked why (product / price / "forgot / didn't see the point") so we fix the real cause.
  • The watch line. A re-order rate well below target is a question, not a verdict: the cohort tells us which driver is biting — subscription, price, forgetting, or the felt gap — before we act. We don't pre-blame the felt gap (the data says it's the smallest of the four), and we never discount or add a gimmick.
Chapter 08

The honest mirror — skin (a sentence, not a SKU)

The one place a real, visible read-out might honestly exist — handled with the same discipline as everything above.

Skin is the body's most visible surface, and over time it can act as an honest read-out of the cellular thesis — not a claim we make, an observation the customer can make. The regulatory firewall is a friend here: an oral skin-outcome claim is an illegal drug claim, so the only legal posture — "an observed read-out, never a promise" — is exactly our honesty discipline. It's a sentence, not a product: a way to talk about what people may notice, never a skin SKU and never implied causation. Any use of this routes through Zone Halo reg review first.

Open — decisions & research items
  • Dose-mirror copy + reg sign-off — the seven touches need final copy, then Zone Halo reg review before they ship. The gram math locks to the final formula (doc 02).
  • Felt-layer viability — whether Rhodiola delivers a perceptible lift is upside, not floor. If it doesn't, L2 + L3 carry retention. Confirm form/dose at the Zone Halo two-day.
  • Churn-driver weights — the ~30 / 25 / 20 / 15 split is one external source. Confirm with a paid lapsed-user report (Mintel / FMCG Gurus) and the founding cohort before re-weighting spend further. Do not demote the felt layer on the strength of one agency blog.
  • The skin read-out language — if used, draft the reg-safe "observed, never promised" wording and clear it before any surface.
  • Re-order target — set the actual re-order threshold the cohort is measured against (feeds doc 07's retained-cycles assumption).
