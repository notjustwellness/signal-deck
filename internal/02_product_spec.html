Chapter 02

Format & packaging Locked

The format is the ritual; the ritual is the product.
  • Form factor: a chew — not a capsule, softgel, or loose powder.
  • Per serving: one chew — up to two for a full dose. One is the goal, but dose-honesty wins: if the accurately-dosed set needs a second chew, we ship two rather than under-dose or overload a single chew.
  • Unit packaging: single-serve, individually packaged daily sachet (sealed; not a tub or bottle).
  • Taste is the priority — wins every tiebreaker except dose-honesty.
  • Natural sweetener system (e.g. allulose). Taste is built on natural sweeteners like allulose — a near-zero-glycemic rare sugar — not a sugar/candy load. A deliberate rebuttal to the "chew = candy, not serious" perception: it keeps the chew pleasant and credible to the efficacy buyer who distrusts gummies. (Final sweetener system locks at Zone Halo.)
  • Branded ingredients only — every active a named, clinically-studied branded form.
The month — visibility is adherence
30 daily packages = one month, kept in a counter vessel built to look good and stay in sight. An empty vessel = a finished month.

Working set = five structural jobs + one felt layer. The felt layer (Rhodiola, in gold) is protected from the minimalism trim — it earns its place on felt-delivery, not outcome-dose. Creatine is deliberately not in the flagship (see Chapter 04): it's the chalky, gram-scale load, and pulling it out protects taste.

Why fewer is more — and why we say so

A short ingredient list reads as "less value" only if we let it. We counter that head-on: every active earns its place by doing a specific job in cellular communication — the signal that lets the cell produce energy — and the set is engineered for little-to-no overlap. Fewer ingredients because each one is load-bearing, not because we cut corners. Kitchen-sink brands stack overlapping actives that compete and dilute; we dose a precise, non-redundant set and tell the customer exactly what each one is for. Precision is the value story — and we make sure the customer knows it.

Chapter 04

The pure-creatine trust SKU Locked

Pure creatine, nothing else — the cleanest deliverable form, by design.
  • Ingredient: pure creatine, 5 g, NNB PurestCreatine — no additives, no flavor, no synergists.
  • Format: dose-sized single-use aluminum tin (~10 ml / 5 g), pre-filled, grab-and-go.
  • Closure: twist-off aluminum cap + sealing liner (dry-seal / creatinine defense; no PVC). No refill-your-own.
  • Stability gate: creatine hydrolyzes to creatinine in moisture — the seal must hold label claim to end of shelf life.
  • Month: 30 tins = one "cellular unit" (one tin a day).
  • Pricing: near-nil margin by design; the return is trust + retention + basket. Not a revenue engine.
  • How it's sold: bundled with the flagship only — never standalone.

Recyclability honesty (locked): no "100% recyclable / reusable / sustainable" claims (recyclable ≠ recycled). Name the liner material; no mono-material claim; no take-back promise.

Chapter 05

Formulation guardrails Locked

The bright lines the formula is built inside.
  • No caffeine anywhere — it blunts creatine, and a stimulant re-captures the energy-drink box we refuse. (Paraxanthine / NMN as a felt substitute: rejected.)
  • Never quietly under-dose. Every trim is disclosed; taste never overrides dose-honesty.
  • Branded forms only, each paired with its named dose when we make a claim. Each claim needs substantiation + Zone Halo reg sign-off.
  • The felt layer is named, never smuggled — Rhodiola is the one deliberately-perceptible active; "no instant hit, builds quietly."

Candidate under evaluation — a B-vitamin (scoped, not a complex)

Noted as a possible addition: a B-complex with all active B forms, incl. TTFD and benfotiamine. Scoped down before it enters the room: not a full complex — "all the Bs" is the most kitchen-sink-coded block in the category, and on the shelf it reads as the exact thing this brand refuses, premium forms or not. The version worth evaluating is one — at most two — mechanistically load-bearing B's, a single form each.

Why it earns a look. B-vitamins are among the most on-thesis cellular-energy cofactors we could name, and they're non-overlapping with the current set: B1 (thiamine) gates pyruvate and α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase — the entry and mid-points of the Krebs cycle; B2→FAD and B3→NAD⁺ run the electron transport chain. Choosing a high-bioavailability form (TTFD or benfotiamine over thiamine HCl) is the Bioavailability pillar in action. Thiamine has the strongest single-active case; riboflavin is the next most defensible.

Gates it must clear at Zone Halo:

  • Redundancy — TTFD and benfotiamine are both vitamin B1. Pick one, never both (the little-to-no-overlap rule).
  • Taste — TTFD is sulfur-based (garlic/sulfur note); does masking hold in a taste-priority chew, or does benfotiamine win on flavor? Taste wins the tiebreaker.
  • Branded-only — confirm a branded form exists for the chosen B (methylfolate/B12 forms do; a branded TTFD is doubtful — may fail this gate).
  • Active-count principle — each B must earn its slot against the <7 set; most won't.
  • B6 safety — chronic high-dose pyridoxine risks peripheral neuropathy; if B6 is included at all, dose conservatively and disclose.
