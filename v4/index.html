The Problem

Chronic skin dryness is not a water problem. It's a structure problem.

You can drink eight glasses of water a day and your skin will still look dry, dull, and depleted. That's because drinking water fixes what's in your bloodstream — it doesn't fix what your skin can hold onto. Skin hydration isn't about intake. It's about the two structural systems your skin uses to retain water.

System 01 — Reservoir

Extracellular Matrix

Your skin holds water in its extracellular matrix, where hyaluronic acid binds up to 1,000 times its weight in water. HA levels in your skin decline roughly 50% by age 50. When the reservoir shrinks, no amount of drinking water restores it.

System 02 — Barrier

Stratum Corneum

Your skin barrier seals water in through a lipid matrix built on ceramides. Ceramide composition declines with age, sun exposure, stress, and dry environments. A compromised barrier lets water evaporate faster than you can replace it.

Both systems decline. Drinking more water doesn't rebuild either one. To fix chronic skin dryness, you have to restore the structures themselves.

Two clinical-dose ingredients. One 90-second ritual. Every day.

signal delivers the two most-studied oral ingredients for skin hydration, each at the exact dose used in the peer-reviewed human trials that prove they work. Packaged in a novel format that doesn't exist anywhere else in the category: a dissolvable chew with a liquid center.
