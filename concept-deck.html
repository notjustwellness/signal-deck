Margin levers

Cost came in low. Premium actives are milligram-dosed (so they cost little); a formulation swap removed the last gram-scale cost line.

Pricing headroom. $89 is the floor; positioning supports $99–120 (AG1 ~$99) — upside on top.

Scale. 10K → 25–50K MOQ pulls per-unit cost down ~20–40%.

Then, in order: lock the formulation cost stack (the cohort needs finished product) · target the already-converted · saturate one beachhead with the cohort's UGC · PR for organic & earned authenticity · paid, aimed narrowly at the already-converted. How we launch — no ghost town

Acquisition spend on an empty storefront is wasted. So before we open to the public, we manufacture day-one density: a founding cohort who actually use N°001 and generate the first reviews and real-world content — so the first visitor meets a living practice, not an empty shelf. We don't scale paid spend until that cohort proves it retains (they reorder). We prime the engine before launch — we don't hope it ignites after.
