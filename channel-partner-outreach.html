What this pack is — and the one rule

Three platforms turn outside authority into proof Signal can't manufacture alone: SuppCo (consumer transparency rating), FrontrowMD (clinician endorsement), Fullscript (clinician distribution). None can be activated before there's a product with a real label and COA — so pre-launch the move is to open the door and build to their bar, not chase the badge.

The one rule for every note below: ask for the process and the requirements, never for a deal. Each is deliberately non-committal — that keeps the DTC-vs-practitioner fork and the certification-cost decisions open until we have real data to decide on. The discipline that matters most is when each one fires.

02Fullscript's published bar — design to it now

Fullscript documents what it requires before any brand reaches its catalog. This is free intelligence: we design the manufacturing agreement to clear it before we ever apply.
What Fullscript looks for verified

Per Fullscript's brand-onboarding documentation (support.fullscript.com — the brand onboarding process):

• 3rd-party cGMP certification by NSF or equivalent (the brand or its contract manufacturer), renewed annually — Fullscript calls this annual-renewal standard "unmatched in the industry."
• Fullscript's own independent third-party lab testing of potency and purity — they verify the bottle, not just the paperwork.
• The Fullscript Quality Assessment — a tiered evaluation that rewards measures beyond the baseline.
• Clinical validation and novel, science-backed ingredients — practitioner-grade expectation.

Why this matters beyond Fullscript: this bar overlaps the SuppCo TrustScore rubric almost exactly (cGMP/NSF facility · third-party potency/purity testing · clinical backing). One procurement decision at the Zone Halo two-day satisfies both platforms at once. likely The single gap that re-appears on both: "clinical validation" — Signal has no clinical study yet. Treat that as a known, named soft spot, not a surprise.

Before any of these go out — three guardrails

1 · Keep "not asking to be listed today" in. It isn't politeness — it protects the DTC-vs-practitioner fork. Deleting it quietly turns an info request into a listing application.

2 · Don't fabricate numbers. Cert and facility costs are Zone Halo's to quote; invented figures undercut the "name the dose" credibility the moment a partner checks them.

3 · The NDA line is optional. A stranger answering a cold web form won't sign one — keep it only when you reach a named human; drop it for the form path.
