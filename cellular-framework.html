The Five Jobs of a Working Cell Making the foundation countable — and interrogable

Signal · INSDR Wellness — the cellcare company The named framework under "build the foundation" 2026-06-13 · draft
Why this exists

"The foundation / the cellular floor" is a metaphor — a skeptic can wave it away and a buyer can't picture it. Young Goose solved the same problem by bolting their thesis to a finite, named, science-anchored framework (their "12 hallmarks of skin aging"). This is Signal's version — but tighter on purpose. Five jobs beat twelve hallmarks: twelve is a credential, five is something a buyer can actually hold in their head and a skeptic can check off one link at a time.

Each job maps to one named active at one named dose, and the credibility of each job is the published evidence behind that ingredient — which is exactly the procurement spec for the supplier asks (see the partner-outreach brief). The framework is the skeleton; the ingredient houses' dossiers are the muscle.

The chain

Read top to bottom — it's a sequence, not a pile. Make energy → protect it → renew the machinery. Each node names a cellular job and what we supply for it, never a result the customer will feel. mechanism claims — likely defensible; all require reg review before consumer use
1
Activate
Magnesium bisglycinate · 200 mg
The cellular job

ATP is only biologically usable bound to magnesium. Magnesium is the required cofactor for ATP utilization (and hundreds of energy-dependent enzyme reactions).

What a skeptic checks → what backs it

Mg-ATP biochemistry is textbook-settled. Ask the magnesium house (e.g. Albion/Balchem TRAACS, if branded) for the chelate bioavailability data. mechanism certain

Consumer-safe line (pre-review)"The active form of cellular energy is magnesium-bound — about 9 of 10 ATP molecules carry magnesium; the rest sit idle."
2
Generate
Kaneka QH® ubiquinol · dose to lock on label
The cellular job

Ubiquinol (the reduced form of CoQ10) is an electron carrier in the mitochondrial electron-transport chain — the chain that actually produces ATP.

What a skeptic checks → what backs it

ETC role is settled; the live questions are form and dose. Ask Kaneka for the ubiquinol clinical dossier + bioavailability vs ubiquinone. strong branded evidence

Consumer-safe line (pre-review)"Ubiquinol ferries the electrons through the chain that produces ATP."
3
Multiply
BioPQQ® · dose to lock on label
The cellular job

PQQ is studied for a role in mitochondrial biogenesis — the cell making new mitochondria (PGC-1α signaling pathway).

What a skeptic checks → what backs it

Biogenesis evidence is the least settled node — keep the language measured ("studied for," not "increases"). Ask the BioPQQ licensor for human data, not just cell/animal. measured

Consumer-safe line (pre-review)"PQQ is studied for its role in mitochondrial biogenesis — the making of new mitochondria."
4
Protect
MitoPrime® ergothioneine · dose to lock on label
The cellular job

Ergothioneine is actively transported into the cell's most vulnerable compartments — including mitochondria — where it acts as an antioxidant.

What a skeptic checks → what backs it

The dedicated transporter (OCTN1) + mitochondrial accumulation is a strong, distinctive story. Ask NNB for the MitoPrime dossier. Avoid the "longevity vitamin" nickname in consumer copy (claim-adjacent). good

5
Renew
Puremidine® spermidine · dose to lock on label
The cellular job

Spermidine is studied for its role in autophagy — the cell's recycling of worn-out components, including damaged mitochondria (mitophagy).

What a skeptic checks → what backs it

Autophagy literature is robust (Madeo et al.); this is the node that closes the loop (clears what nodes 2–4 wear out). Ask Oxford Healthspan / the Puremidine house for the dossier + sourcing story. strong literature

Consumer-safe line (pre-review)"Spermidine is studied for its role in autophagy — how the cell recycles its own worn-out machinery."
6
Reserve — the sixth node, inside N°001
Creatine · 5 g · folded into N°001
The cellular job

The phosphocreatine system buffers ATP — rapidly recharging it for surge-demand moments (creatine kinase shuttle).

Why it's the reserve, not a base job

It's the reserve tank — a surge buffer layered on top of the five base jobs, not one of them. Originally scoped as a separate SKU; as of 2026-06-13 it's folded into the single product (N°001), the sixth node in the same box. Not an upsell, not a second SKU. creatine = most-studied ingredient

Consumer-safe line (pre-review)"Creatine recharges the phosphocreatine system that buffers ATP for rapid-demand moments."

The rules that keep it a moat, not a claim

  1. Names jobs, never outcomes. "Make energy, protect it, renew the machinery" is cell biology. "More energy / sharper focus / age slower" is the forbidden outcome claim. The framework lives exactly one level below the outcome — same fence as everything else.
  2. One job, one named active, one named dose. If a node can't name its dose, it's fairy dust. This is "real doses, not fairy dust" made structural.
  3. Five, not twelve — on purpose. Tighter is the edge. A buyer holds five; a skeptic checks five. Don't let it sprawl.
  4. Each node's credibility = the supplier's dossier. The framework tells you exactly which study library to request from which house. Build the framework, then go ask (see partner-outreach brief).
  5. "Necessary, not sufficient" still governs. The five jobs are what the cell needs to run; the framework supplies the inputs. It never promises the customer a felt result.

Doses on nodes 2–5 are placeholders until the formulation locks. This artifact is the skeleton; reg review decides which words survive to the label.
The one move

The framework is the procurement spec for credibility

You're not inventing science — you're organizing borrowed evidence into a map a skeptic can walk. Lock these five (six) jobs, and each node becomes a precise request to an ingredient house: "here is the job your ingredient does in our framework — send me the studies and the seal that prove it." The framework turns five vendor relationships into five credibility partnerships.
