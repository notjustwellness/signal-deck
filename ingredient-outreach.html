Gate zero — answered, and it changes the whole play

Signal sources its actives through Zone Halo, and Zone Halo is a company under NNB — both owned by Jake & Sean. So this is not five cold outreaches. It splits in two: Track A — NNB's own ingredients (MitoPrime®, PurestCreatine®) — where you don't write a letter, you write a contract term, because they already hold a $0.67/unit royalty and want you to sell. Track B — the third-party actives (Kaneka, BioPQQ, Puremidine) that ZH sources for you — where you have no direct line, so ZH must either broker the rights or introduce you.

And one thing to manage, not ignore: Jake & Sean now capture value at three layers — ingredient margin, formulation, and the royalty. Aligned incentives, but it puts a cost-transparency blind spot and a related-party diligence flag on the table. See §04.

02Track ANNB's own ingredients — negotiate it, don't ask for it

MitoPrime + PurestCreatine are NNB's. You hold royalty leverage — they only earn the $0.67 if Signal sells, so making these nodes credible is in their interest. Don't send a polite letter; put it on the engagement term sheet. leverage is highest pre-signature
  1. Dossiers + seal + co-marketing, bundled into the engagement.The MitoPrime / PurestCreatine study libraries, the seal/"contains" rights, and co-branded content — as deliverables of the relationship, not separate favors. They have every reason to say yes.
  2. Sean as the named, citable scientific voice.If Sean is the formulator, he's your built-in authority — already aligned by the royalty. The "scientist as a citable voice / podcast guest" play needs no cold recruiting; it's a person you're already paying to work on Signal. This is the no-host authority engine, handed to you.
  3. Pre-approved claim language for both ingredients.NNB's reg-vetted wording for ergothioneine + creatine keeps your framework nodes inside DSHEA. Get it in writing.

04The related-party flag — manage it, don't paper over it

Jake & Sean take three bites — ingredient margin (NNB) + formulation (ZH) + the $0.67/unit royalty. Aligned, but it carries two real risks and a diligence flag. structure certain

Cost-transparency blind spot likely

When ZH sources Kaneka/BioPQQ/Puremidine for you, you see ZH's price, not the house's. They could mark up the third-party actives and you couldn't easily tell — and your hero COGS model (~$19–27, with Kaneka ubiquinol alone ~$6–11 of it) rests on that number. Ask for itemized third-party ingredient costs, or accept a related-party blind spot in your margin.

The supplier-funded-study counter likely

Two nodes (Protect, Reserve) cite NNB's own studies — and NNB is your formulator-with-a-royalty. A sharp Stacker will say "the company selling you the ingredient funded the research." The counter is the per-lot third-party testing already in scope (~$2,500/lot) — it proves identity + potency independent of NNB's efficacy claims. Lean on it.

Investor diligence will circle this likely

One related party at three layers of the P&L is a standard diligence question. Get ahead of it: document arm's-length pricing, the itemized costs, and why the embedded-R&D-arm structure is a feature (aligned incentives) not a leak.

The one move

Negotiate credibility into the engagement, not after it

Everything here — the NNB dossiers and seals, Sean as your citable scientist, ZH brokering the Kaneka/BioPQQ/Puremidine rights, and itemized third-party costs — should land on the two-day engagement term sheet, while your leverage is highest and before anything is signed. The framework already names what each ingredient must prove; this brief names who delivers it and on which track. Don't leave it for a "later" that arrives after the ink dries.
