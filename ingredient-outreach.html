01What you're actually asking for — the menu

You currently ask one thing: price per kg. That's the COGS relationship. These five are the credibility relationship — the branded-ingredient "partner program" most houses run. programs likely; confirm per house
  1. The clinical dossier + the right to cite it. highest valueTheir published human studies on the ingredient are the trial you don't have to run. Ask for the full study library, the substantiation dossier, and their pre-approved claim language — so you stay inside DSHEA using their legal-vetted wording, per framework node.
  2. The seal + "contains X®" license. baselineThe right to put Kaneka Ubiquinol™ / MitoPrime® / BioPQQ® / Puremidine® on pack and site. Baseline trust transfer. Ask: licensing terms, qualifying dose/format, and the claim-approval process.
  3. Co-marketing / market-development funds (MDF). often co-fundedMany houses co-fund marketing for brands featuring their ingredient: co-branded one-pagers, webinars, social assets, sometimes shared spend or volume rebates. Ask flatly whether a program exists for a launch brand making their ingredient a hero at a clinical dose.
  4. Their scientist as a citable voice. authorityA chief scientist or sponsored KOL who'll give a quote, run a webinar, or be a guest on someone else's podcast — the no-host version of borrowed authority. Connects straight to the guesting strategy.
  5. Featured-brand / case-study placement. inboundBeing listed on their "brands that use us" page = inbound credibility + traffic. Offer to be a flagship launch case study; that's often the trade for the co-marketing in #3.

04Your leverage — and the strings

Why they say yes: you're making their ingredient the hero at a clinical dose in a premium brand — the exact showcase they want, and the opposite of the under-dosed brands that embarrass them. Premium positioning lifts their ingredient's brand too. You are the case study they'd pay to have. likely

String 1 — claim-approval control certain

The seal and dossier come with the house policing how you use them. Good for DSHEA discipline; it means you can't improvise claims. Build that into the reg-review loop, don't fight it.

String 2 — the seal is table stakes, not a moat certain

Every competitor using Kaneka can show the same mark. Seals are scaffolding on top of your moat (the framework + the honesty), not the moat itself. Don't let Signal become "a Kaneka brand."

String 3 — the ZH relationship question (again) likely

If you don't hold the direct supplier relationship, none of the above is yours to ask for. Gate zero isn't optional — it decides whether this brief is actionable at all.

The sequence

Framework → dossier request → seal + co-marketing

The Five Jobs framework already tells you the job each ingredient does — so each letter writes itself: name the node, ask for the evidence and seal that prove it, offer the showcase in return. Five vendor relationships become five credibility partnerships. But send nothing until gate zero is answered.
