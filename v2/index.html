IP & Defensibility

The provisional utility patent. File it first.

Patents matter less than you think for consumer brands — Grüns sold for $1.2B with no meaningful patents. Liquid Death, LMNT, AG1 don't rely on IP either. But when an acquirer runs due diligence, the IP column on their spreadsheet needs a check mark. The provisional utility patent is that check mark.

Hero Action

Provisional Utility Patent

Cost: ~$3-5K
Timeline: Filed before any public deck sharing, investor pitch, or manufacturer briefing.

Claim: the combined formulation + dual-pathway delivery system. Lipid-carrier micro-emulsion for fat-soluble actives + xylitol-MCC matrix for water-soluble delivery + engineered 90-120s dissolution timeline.

Buys 12 months of priority date protection. Converts to full non-provisional patent if the concept proves out. Lets lapse if not.

Low risk. High optionality. Files the priority date at a fraction of the cost of a full patent. This is the check mark every acquirer wants to see.

Design Patent (Packet)

Cost: ~$2-5K

Protects the ornamental appearance: matte Signal front, clear back revealing 4 chews, embossed lowercase wordmark, square condom-packet format.

Narrower than utility but cheaper and faster. Makes copycats pick between "changing the design" or "risking infringement."

Trade Secret

Cost: $0
Duration: Forever (as long as secret).

Never patent: exact xylitol crystalline spec, MCC ratios, MCT emulsion recipe, flavor coating system, supplier relationships. Keep them confidential in perpetuity.

This is the actual moat. Patents become public and are designed around. Trade secrets stay private forever.

The real moat isn't patents — it's brand, habit, and distribution. But a $3-5K provisional filing closes the biggest hole in an acquirer's due-diligence checklist. Low cost. High signal. File it first.
