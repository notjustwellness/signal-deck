The logline — read before the shot sheet

Every supplement on the shelf sells you the moment: the focused mind, the recovered body, the glow. This film shows the energy coiling toward that moment — across creature after creature, each one loading, gathering, compressing — and then it never lets the release happen. The payoff every competitor promises is the one thing we refuse to show, because the payoff was never ours to promise. The withholding is "we name the dose, not the outcome."

Everyone shows you the moment. We build the energy that earns it.

No narration. The film works on the body, not the ear: an ascending coil with accelerating cuts and a rising, unresolved sound, building a charge it deliberately won't spend. The energy has nowhere to go but into the person watching. The goal is a single felt state — primed. Ready to go. An athlete should be able to watch this and feel it in their legs. The only thing in 60 seconds that's allowed to resolve is the brand.

How to use this — the division of labor
  • The droppable file is assets/signal_video_prompts.txt — paste one shot prompt per generation into your AI video tool (Runway Gen-4, Kling, Veo, Sora, Pika). This HTML is the human-readable brief behind it.
  • AI generates the coil clips. You finish in an editor. The whole film lives in the edit rhythm and the sound — neither of which AI generates. Post does: the accelerating match-cuts, the tension bed + gun, and the exact wordmark overlay. The clips are raw material; the priming is assembled.
  • The wordmark and sound are real files, supplied — not prompts. Don't ask the AI to draw the logo or guess the sound. See the asset manifest (§7).
  • Workflow: generate clips → pick the most coiled takes → cut to an accelerating rhythm → lay the rising tension bed → deny the release → fire the gun + wordmark → export :60 + cutdowns.

The framework — what we show, what we withhold

Every outcome a competitor sells has a moment in nature where energy culminates toward it. We show the culmination — the coil — and cut away before the moment arrives. The withheld moment is the implied outcome we refuse to promise. This film uses only the high-arousal (sympathetic) outcomes — focus, power, launch — because they build the "ready to go" charge. The restful ones (recovery, glow, longevity) would deflate the coil; they belong to a different film.

Span domains, not just the gym. The athlete is the clearest example of the feeling, not the target market. Keep one non-athletic human at a threshold (the stage, the lift, the leap) so the film reads "primed for anything," not as a pre-workout ad.

2 · The match-cut spine

Two devices carry the film with no words. First, cut on the pulse, not the subject — the amber-red glow is the only constant, so each cut says "same fire, different creature." Second, the cut rhythm accelerates — that quickening is what the body reads as a coil tightening.

Cut on the pulse — and let the rhythm tighten
stillness (focus)→the load (power)→compression (salmon)→the edge (flock)→the gather (crouch)→the human (held breath)→the stack→DENY→the gun

The connective tissue — a pulsing, vein-like glow — is the single most important thing to get right and the one thing AI/stock can't fully supply. Treat it as a designed motion element (abstract bioluminescent network) composited at each cut, so the "same fire" reads as literally the same fire across owl, salmon, flock and human. And the wordmark's snap-to-one at the close uses that same glow language — so the only release in the film resolves out of the same fire it spent 57 seconds refusing to spend.

3 · The sonic engine — the tension bed & the gun

The brand's entrainment mechanic (two tones → unison at A4) is the same shape as this film, so we use it as the tension itself: hold the tones apart for the whole film (an unresolved, beating, rising bed) and let them snap to unison only at the end — the single release, a starting gun. Two real reference WAVs are supplied.

The two sonic assets
The bed
assets/signal_tension_bed.wav (~12s) — two tones held ~10 Hz apart, beating and never resolving, slowly creeping up in pitch and volume, over an accelerating heartbeat (~60→140 bpm). This is the coil. Time/cut it under the whole film; it must feel like it needs to resolve and won't.
The gun
assets/signal_sound.wav (~1.4s) — the tones snap to unison at A4 with a low transient at the lock. The film's only release. Lay it over the black at 0:57, timed to the wordmark snap.
The mechanic
The brand calls it entrainment — the body's pull toward sync. In a film that denies every other release, the entrainment lock becomes the sole exhale: the brand is the resolution nature was never given.
The wordmark
Two wordmark ghosts at a slight horizontal offset snap into perfect overlap on the gun (not a slow drift). On-dark asset: assets/signal_wordmark_on_dark.png (transparent, 1557×348) + .svg.
Delivery
Loudness-normalize the final mix to −14 LUFS; export MP3/AAC. Regenerate/tune both files via assets/gen_signal_sound.py.
Two deliberate deviations from canon — brand-owner calls
  • Snap, not fade. Canon says the sound "becomes clear exactly as it fades" — soft, a sunset. This brief wants the close to snap — a trigger, a starting gun. Sharper than canon, on purpose.
  • Continuous bed, not a single sting. Canon says one sonic event, never mid-roll. Here the detuned tones run as a continuous rising bed that resolves once at the end. Argued as one continuous element resolving a single time (not a repeated chime) — but it is a real deviation. If you'd rather stay strict, drop the bed and use only the gun.
  • Monaural beat, not binaural. Canon says "binaural." Both WAVs render a monaural beat (both tones, both channels) so the ~10 Hz beating is audible on speakers, not just headphones. BINAURAL=True in the generator flips it.
  • The priming-integrity line. The film primes the viewer through edit and sound — legitimate, and the brand's emotional turf. It must never imply the product creates that charge (that's the manufactured-sensation trap canon bans). The readiness is the brand's world, not a product effect.

4 · The rule that protects the brand

Non-negotiable — this is what keeps the film on-brand and legal
  • The product never enters the cause-and-effect chain. No sachet beside a hatching egg, no chew dissolving into a blooming flower. The instant Signal appears as the cause of a natural miracle, the film makes the exact outcome claim the brand is built to never make.
  • Nature carries the awe; the product stays honest. If the product appears at all, it appears after the wordmark close, separately — as the named-dose floor, not the magic behind the bloom.
  • No narration, no claim. The film argues with edit and sound only. The single on-screen line is the tagline — never "supports," "boosts," "improves," or a benefit.
  • Prime the viewer, don't promise the product. The charge comes from the film, and attaches to the brand's world — never to an implied product effect (that's the manufactured-sensation trap canon bans). The selling happens downstream, on a surface the film points to (§5).

5 · The ending fork — what the last frame asks for

The film's CTA depends on a decision that isn't a film decision

If a public consumer surface exists: final frame is wordmark + sound + a URL. The film becomes a top-of-funnel conversion asset — awe → door → product.

If it doesn't: the film can only end on the wordmark. It is pure brand atmosphere with nowhere to send anyone — beautiful, and strategically stranded.

That fork is the company-to-pitch vs. product-to-sell question. Resolve that before committing real production budget — otherwise you've shot a gorgeous top-of-funnel asset for a funnel with no bottom.

6 · Production path — cheapest proof first

Don't greenlight an original shoot to test a feeling. Validate the edit grammar with footage that already exists; spend on originals only for the beats stock can't cover.

Phase 1 · The AI supercut test

~$0–50 · a weekend

Generate the coil clips from the prompt pack (§7), cut to the accelerating rhythm, lay the supplied tension bed + gun + wordmark. Goal: does the denied release leave a test viewer primed — restless, ready to move? If it works with AI footage, the concept is real.

Decision gate: do people feel it in their body? If the denial reads as anticlimax instead of charge, the cut rhythm is wrong — fix it before spending a cent more.

Phase 2 · The sonic + originals

composer + selective shoot

Finalize the signal sound (normalize the supplied WAV to −14 LUFS + ambient bed). Upgrade the beats AI can't carry to brand-look: the designed pulse/glow transition asset and the human-turn moments (real shoot or higher-fidelity gen).

Gate: only after Phase 1 proves the cut, AND §5's funnel question is answered.

Phase 3 · Master + cutdowns

finishing

Color, sound mix, the wordmark snap in finishing. Deliver :60 master + :30 / :15 / :06 cutdowns and vertical 9:16 reframes for paid + organic.

Cutdowns reuse the same coil — the :06 is one coil + the denial + the gun.

7 · AI prompt pack & asset manifest

The full, copy-paste prompts live in assets/signal_video_prompts.txt — that's the file you drop into the AI platform. Below: the global style fragment to prepend to every shot, and the companion asset files.

Global style bible — prepend to EVERY shot (consistency is the hard part)

Cinematic macro nature-documentary look. Extreme close-up, shallow depth of field, anamorphic, photoreal, fine 35mm film grain. Palette: deep espresso-brown shadows, soft bone-cream highlights, and a single warm oxblood-red internal glow (#AD4236) that pulses from inside living tissue like bioluminescent energy. Low-key dramatic lighting, volumetric light, deep negative space. Every shot is a "moment before" — held tension, coiled potential energy, on the verge of an explosive action that never comes. Slow, taut, charged motion (NOT relaxed). The red-amber internal pulse is the through-line — it must appear, subtly, in every shot.

Global negative prompt: text, captions, watermark, logo, brand name, the action itself (no strike, no leap, no flight, no release), relaxed/calm posture, distorted anatomy, extra fingers, mutated hands, cartoon, plastic CGI look, oversaturated, neon, shaky cam.

Assembly notes — what the AI can't do for you
  • Rhythm is the film. Cut on the pulse, not the subject, and make holds shorten beat to beat (≈10s → sub-second). The acceleration is the priming; if the cut rhythm is flat, nothing else saves it.
  • Color: AI clips won't match out of the box — grade them all to one look (espresso shadows, bone highlights, the single oxblood glow). Re-add the internal glow as a masked comp where a clip lost it.
  • Audio: lay signal_tension_bed.wav under the whole film (it rises and never resolves); cut it dead into ~2s of near-silence at the denial (0:55); then fire signal_sound.wav (the gun) over black at 0:57. Final mix to −14 LUFS.
  • Wordmark close: place the PNG as two horizontally-offset copies; snap them to perfect overlap on the gun's transient (a hard cut/quick lock, not a slow drift). Tagline beneath, then cut to black.
