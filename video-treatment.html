The logline — read before the shot sheet

In nature, some creatures don't just live — they signal. A firefly, a wave of plankton, a creature in the deep send a flash of living light into the dark. We see the flash. We never see what it costs: that light is made by cellular energy — the same fire in every living thing — poured out in a single instant of communication.

You see the signal. You never see what it took to send it.

No narration. The film lives in the dark — where the energy gathers — and lets brief flashes reveal the world for a heartbeat before it goes black again. The contrast holds your eye; the darkness holds the story. Once, we go inside a flash to show the furious cellular machinery behind one quiet blink. A creature spends everything it has to send a single signal — and so do you, every moment, unseen. And the brand becomes the one signal that doesn't go dark.

How to use this — the division of labor
  • The droppable file is assets/signal_video_prompts.txt — paste one shot prompt per generation into your AI video tool (Runway Gen-4, Kling, Veo, Sora, Pika). This HTML is the human-readable brief behind it.
  • AI generates two kinds of clips — bioluminescent flashes (exterior, in the dark) and the interior cellular machinery. You finish in an editor. The film lives in the darkness, the flash timing, and the sound — none of which AI hands you. Post owns: the black, the flash rhythm, the charge-up builds, the slow-entrainment bed, and the wordmark.
  • The wordmark and sound are real files, supplied — not prompts. Don't ask the AI to draw the logo or guess the sound. See the asset manifest (§7).
  • Workflow: generate flashes (firefly / plankton / deep-sea) + interior cellular clips → assemble against long stretches of black → build the charge before each flash → go inside once for the cost → lay the slow-entrainment bed → end on the brand flash that stays lit → export :60 + cutdowns.

The framework — the signal & its cost

Each creature gives off a signal you can see — and we make it brief on purpose, because the real subject is the energy it cost, which you normally never see. We show the flash, then weight the whole film toward the dark, where the energy gathers. The light is the creature communicating — not a health benefit — so it's safe to show; what we want felt is the cost underneath it.

Weight the film to the dark. The flashes are seconds; the darkness — the charging, the cost, the recovery — is the rest. The disproportion of dark-to-light is the argument: the signal is the tip, the energy is everything underneath. The light is the creature's signal, never a stand-in for a human benefit (that would be a claim).

2 · The spine — dark, flash, and the cost

The film rests on a contrast and a reveal. The contrast: long darkness punctuated by brief flashes — the dark is the energy gathering, the flash is the signal. The reveal: once, we go inside a flash to show the cellular cost, so the audience understands every flash was paid for. The amber-red interior glow is the thread that ties firefly, plankton, deep-sea creature and human into one fire.

Dark holds the energy · flash is the signal · inside is the cost
black·flash·charge (dark)·flash⟿INSIDE the cost·aftercost (dark)⟿the turn (it's you)·the lasting light

The flashes can be real footage (fireflies, plankton, deep-sea) — the one thing AI/stock can't supply is the inside: the cellular machinery pouring energy into the light, and the seamless match between that machinery and the human interior. Build it as a designed motion element (amber-red bioluminescent network) so the firefly's cost and yours read as the same fire. The brand close uses that same glow — the one signal that, unlike the creatures', stays lit.

3 · The sonic engine — the slow entrainment

The slow-entrainment sound is the charge in the dark: two tones begin barely apart and drift toward unison at A4 across the whole 60 seconds — energy gathering, never rushing — over a warm, steady heartbeat. The brief flashes get soft accents in the mix; the tones reach unison only at the brand — the one signal that stays lit. Generated: assets/signal_meditation.wav (full ~60s, 4 Hz→unison A4, steady 60 bpm).

The sound — one slow convergence
The bed
A warm sustained drone: two tones ~3–5 Hz apart, drifting almost imperceptibly toward each other over the full minute, never rushing. A slow, steady heartbeat (~60 bpm, constant) underneath — patience, not pursuit.
The arrival
At the wordmark (0:57) the two tones reach unison at A4 and settle. The mark clears exactly as the sound resolves — "the logo becomes clear exactly as the sound fades." A soft landing, the recognition exhaling.
The mechanic
The brand calls it entrainment — the body's pull toward sync. Here it's literal: across the film the sound slowly comes into agreement with itself, the same way the viewer slowly realizes the energy is theirs.
The wordmark
Two wordmark ghosts at a slight offset drift gently into perfect overlap (not a snap). On-dark asset: assets/signal_wordmark_on_dark.png (transparent, 1557×348) + .svg.
Delivery

4 · The rule that protects the brand

Non-negotiable — this is what keeps the film on-brand and legal
  • The product never enters the cause-and-effect chain. No product shown causing a flash or any natural event. The instant Signal appears as the cause of the light, the film makes the exact outcome claim the brand is built to never make.
  • The light is the creature's signal — never a human benefit. A firefly communicating is safe to show; "the glow = your vitality/energy/glow" is a structure-function claim. The light belongs to the animal; the only thing we tie to the viewer is the shared cellular energy, shown as cost, never as a promised result.
  • No narration, no claim. The film works through image and sound only. The only on-screen words are "You see the signal. Not what it costs to send it," "You don't start it, you feed it," and the tagline — never "supports," "boosts," "improves," or a benefit.
  • Not a stimulant — the foundation. Weighting the film to the dark/cost (not the flash) says Signal is the foundation underneath energy, not a burst of it. "You feed it" is the firewall against the energy-drink read: the energy is already yours; Signal feeds the floor it runs on. The selling happens downstream, on a surface the film points to (§5).

5 · The ending fork — what the last frame asks for

The film's CTA depends on a decision that isn't a film decision

If a public consumer surface exists: final frame is wordmark + sound + a URL. The film becomes a top-of-funnel conversion asset — awe → door → product.

If it doesn't: the film can only end on the wordmark. It is pure brand atmosphere with nowhere to send anyone — beautiful, and strategically stranded.

That fork is the company-to-pitch vs. product-to-sell question. Resolve that before committing real production budget — otherwise you've shot a gorgeous top-of-funnel asset for a funnel with no bottom.

6 · Production path — cheapest proof first

Don't greenlight an original shoot to test a feeling. Validate the edit grammar with footage that already exists; spend on originals only for the beats stock can't cover.

Phase 1 · The AI supercut test

~$0–50 · a weekend

Generate the flashes + the interior-cost clips from the prompt pack (§7), assemble against black with charge-up builds, lay the slow-entrainment bed, end on the brand flash. Goal: does the dark/flash contrast grab, and does the inside-the-flash cost land — "look what it took"? If it works with AI/stock footage, the concept is real.

Decision gate: do the flashes pull the eye, and does the cost register? If it reads as "pretty lights," you're worshipping the flash — re-weight to the dark before spending more.

Phase 2 · The sonic + originals

composer + selective shoot

Finalize the signal sound (normalize the supplied WAV to −14 LUFS + ambient bed). Upgrade the beats AI can't carry to brand-look: the designed pulse/glow transition asset and the human-turn moments (real shoot or higher-fidelity gen).

Gate: only after Phase 1 proves the cut, AND §5's funnel question is answered.

Phase 3 · Master + cutdowns

finishing

Color, sound mix, the wordmark drift in finishing. Deliver :60 master + :30 / :15 / :06 cutdowns and vertical 9:16 reframes for paid + organic.

Cutdowns reuse the same descent — the :06 is one interior + the turn + the soft arrival.

Global style bible — prepend to EVERY shot (consistency is the hard part)

Two looks, one palette. Exterior flashes: photoreal bioluminescence in near-total darkness — a firefly in dense forest, plankton in a breaking wave, a deep-sea glow — the light brief and soft, revealing surroundings for a heartbeat, deep black all around. Interior (the cost): extreme cellular close-up, the light organ / machinery pouring a warm oxblood-red glow (#AD4236) into the reaction. Across both: deep espresso-black, bone highlights, the single oxblood-amber glow as the through-line. Low-key, volumetric, lots of negative space. Slow, calm, reverent (NOT fast).

Global negative prompt: text, captions, watermark, logo, brand name, bright/daytime scenes, full lighting, the creature's whole body in clear view for long, human glowing, fast cutting, distorted anatomy, cartoon, plastic CGI look, oversaturated, garish neon, shaky cam.

Assembly notes — what the AI can't do for you
  • The dark is the film. Cut most of the runtime to near-black. Flashes are seconds; let the black breathe between them. The disproportion of dark-to-light is the whole argument — don't fill the silence with light.
  • The charge-up: before a flash, ramp a faint glow building in the black (a few seconds), then the flash, then dark. That build is where you make the energy felt; don't just hard-pop the flashes.
  • Inside-the-flash (the cost): on one flash, dissolve through the light into the cellular machinery (oxblood-amber glow). Grade it so it matches the human-interior clip exactly — then at the turn, let the same machinery read as human. No announced cut; a slow dawning.
  • Audio: the slow-entrainment bed under the whole film (two tones drifting to unison, steady ~60 bpm heartbeat); soft accents on each flash; the tones settle to unison only at the brand. Final mix to −14 LUFS.
  • The brand flash: the wordmark rises like a flash but holds — unlike the creatures', it doesn't go dark. Two offset PNG copies drift into one as the sound resolves, the line beneath, slow fade.
