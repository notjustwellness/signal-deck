Prologue · The origin

why signal exists.

The brand wasn’t reverse-engineered from a market gap. It was built from the founder’s reckoning — the canonical telling, and the source every customer-facing cut derives from. Source of truth · founder_story.md

My father had a stroke ten years ago. He’s still here. But when his eyes open and I look in, no one looks back — just an abyss. I watch the signal go dark in him a little more every time.

Now I’m watching it work through my mother. Diabetes. A stroke. Then dementia. Her light’s still on — it just shines a little differently now.

When it’s the people who raised you, you do something. So I bought everything — first for them, grasping at every promise on every label. Then, afraid of what I might be carrying myself, I started buying it for me too. A whole cabinet of it. One morning I stood in front of all of it and realized I had no idea if any of it was working. I was running on hope and blind faith.

Maybe you know that cabinet. The bottles you can’t remember starting. The quiet suspicion you’re spending money to feel like you’re doing something — not to actually do it.

I’m the kind of person who can’t take a label at its word — I have to read the science underneath it. So I did. And the deeper I went, the more obvious the mistake became: everything in that cabinet was chasing an outcome. Nothing was feeding the foundation those outcomes are built on — the cellular layer underneath all of it. The energy the body makes before it can do anything else. And the low, constant inflammation that chokes that system in almost all of us now — and a little more every year.

Chasing outcomes is the trap. So I stopped chasing. I started underneath — priming the system everything else depends on, so whatever you do next actually lands.

That’s signal. Not another promise. A practice. One signal a day. It compounds.

You can’t always stop the dark. You can keep sending the signal. Send yours.

How this may be used. The parent lines describe the founder’s family — never the product. Implying signal prevents, slows, or treats stroke, dementia, or diabetes is an illegal disease claim (DSHEA/FDA): emotional truth, never medical claim. Lives in owned founder-voice surfaces only (About, founder letter) — never paid-ad creative. The kernel “the signal go dark” is verbatim, present tense, said once. The inflammation / cellular lines are the founder’s general reasoning — never a claim signal reduces inflammation or improves cellular signaling; reg-review before any public surface. 02 · The wordmark

one mark. four versions.

The visible wordmark is the word “signal” rendered as its International Phonetic Alphabet transcription — lowercase, set in brackets like a dictionary entry.

The IPA transcription is a brand move that says “we know what we are; we don’t need to translate ourselves.” The brackets make it unmistakable as notation, not text. Where other brands rely on a stylized logotype, signal uses the very notation language teachers use to teach the word itself.

four approved versions

The canonical wordmark is traced from the brand photograph — a true phonetic serif (single-story g, turned-alpha ɑ, the length mark ː), spaced brackets, the simplified form / sɪgnɑːl /. The brackets carry the one hit of Signal Red; the letters are bone. Within the letters, s ɪ g n sits a hair brighter than the a ː l tail — at a glance it reads signal; on a second look, sign.

do. don’t.

Do

  • Use canonical two-color (A) as default on dark + color print.
  • Use the traced serif wordmark as artwork — never re-typeset it in a system font.
  • Keep the wordmark lowercase, even sentence-initial.
  • Add aria-label="signal" to every web instance so screen readers and copy-paste return the brand name.
  • Respect clear space = one lowercase “s” all sides.
  • Keep the IPA brackets — they are part of the mark, and they carry the one red.

Don’t

  • Re-typeset the mark — the letterforms are fixed traced vector, not a live font.
  • Capitalize the wordmark or any character.
  • Rotate, skew, outline, drop-shadow, or distort it.
  • Recolor the letters outside the sepia bone range.
  • Place on busy photographic backgrounds — use a solid surface.
  • Strip the brackets or the length mark (ː).

the monogram — /s/

For surfaces too small for the full IPA wordmark — favicon, app icon, social avatar, a letterhead mark — the wordmark reduces to /s/: the real serif “s” from the wordmark, flanked by the brackets that carry the one red. Same logic as the wordmark’s spaced brackets.

one hue. four values. one red.

A warm sepia monochrome drawn straight from the brand photograph — espresso to bone, all on a single warm axis. Cinematic, hushed, adult. Then one disciplined accent: Signal Red, used once per surface and never twice.

signal red
#AD4236 · rgb(173, 66, 54)
The single hit — one per surface, never two. On a surface with the wordmark, red is the brackets. On a packet, red is the SKU index number. On web, red is the one CTA. It is a signal, not a default — keep it rare and it stays loud.

what we never use

Never pure white #FFFFFF — too clinical; use Bone. Never pure black #000000 — too cold; use Espresso. Never a second hue — the field is monochrome sepia; the only non-sepia is the single Signal-Red accent. Never two reds on one surface. Never pastels, kraft, or neon. No gradients except the photographic vignette + grain that give the system its film feel. signal_brand_identity.html p.04 — revised, B-hybrid

three fonts. one job each.

One display face for headlines, one editorial face for overlines, one body face for everything you read. The wordmark stands apart — a traced serif drawn from the photograph, not a system font.

05 · Voice & tone

quiet confidence. stated, not sold.

One-line voice: signal talks like the friend who walks into every room already in their body — and somehow makes you feel like you’re in yours too. Not arrogant. Not guru. Not coach. Not influencer. signal states. It doesn’t persuade.

the five values

Equally weighted. None compensates for another. If a piece fails one, it fails all five. Authenticity is not a sixth value. It is what the other five make.

Clarity

A reader should know what we’re saying after one line. We earn attention by being clear, not clever.

If a sentence needs a second read, cut it in half.

Usefulness

Every piece teaches something. We don’t post. We explain.

Six seconds, one thing learned.

Honesty

The phone in their pocket is a lie detector. We stop one sentence before the line where someone could check us and find air.

If you’d cringe seeing it next to the evidence, don’t ship it.

Consistency

Same brand in a DM as on a billboard. The compounding is the proof.

Consistency is the new persuasion.

Earned Authority

A brand can’t claim its way into credibility. We don’t announce that we’re trusted. We show up until we are.

Authority is given. We earn the right to receive it.

AI is plumbing, not voice — for now

Today, the original idea, sentence, and frame come from a human first. AI organizes, retrieves, accelerates, and refines — it doesn’t originate our copy, speak for the brand, or sit between us and the consumer. But this is a stance, not dogma. As AI evolves, so does the way we use it; we don’t hold a hard line for its own sake. We look for the most optimized fit for AI in the work, and we revisit that fit as the tools change.

Human-led today. The fit evolves.

the three owned objections

Category-creation brands earn trust by owning legitimate skepticism, not deflecting it. The discipline is to write the answer first — well enough that the customer quotes us back to their own skeptical friend.

“just eat real food.”

The most-upvoted reflexive reply to any supplement post. We agree with the priorities — food first. The version worth answering is that supplements are unnecessary because food covers it.

Canonical answer: “Eat real food. Take real doses.”

Closer register: “Wash your face. Eat real food. Take signal. Three daily practices that don’t replace each other.”

“maybe i don’t actually need supplements.”

The honest tradeoff. The brand is willing to tell a customer their numbers are fine and to fix sleep instead — the discipline of selling foundation over output.

Canonical answer: “Maybe you don’t. Most supplements chase a problem — foundation is the one thing worth taking before you have one.”

Closer register: “You might be right, and we’ll say so — if your sleep, food, and movement are handled, that’s the foundation, and you may not need us. Most people’s aren’t.”

project_formulator_synthesis · playbook §IX working set

“shouldn’t this be personalized to me?”

The testing / personalization objection. Out-reason, don’t out-test. The category’s newest move sells a blood panel next to a blend of dozens of undisclosed-dose ingredients — a band-aid over an output: you may feel better for a while, but walk away no smarter about your body. You don’t need a test to know your cells need their substrate. The foundation is the proof — and the case against testing rests on the undisclosed doses, not the ingredient count.

Codex Ch 01 §2 · Ch 02 §4 (third owned objection)

Register: the codex (May 2026) governs. “send the right signal” is retained as the master tagline; earlier v5-era lines (“90 seconds”, “invincible”) are retired.

07 · Product & naming

foundation first. everything stacks.

The line is numbered, not named — N°001 / N°002 / N°003. The number is the product noun; the role is the register.

Mapping per current corpus: N°001 = the foundation (multi-active dual-chew flagship · one substrate chew + one keystone chew, ~8 actives) · N°002 = the daily fuel (ATP-machinery, two pearl chews) · N°003 = the evening practice (overnight maintenance, two chews). Launch order: N°001 Q4 2026 · N°002 2027 · N°003 2028. Codex Ch 04 §1–§4 · manifesto §IV

ingredient philosophy

Signal sits at Layer 1 — the substrate: the cellular machinery (mitochondrial biogenesis, autophagy, electron-transport cofactors, redox defense) that Layers 2–4 (foundation nutrients, specialists, acute) all depend on. “Signal is not in competition with other supplements. Signal is the substrate those other supplements depend on.”

Two formulation disciplines are load-bearing: branded actives only — every active is a registered ingredient brand with its own regulatory dossier and study set, never a commodity spot-market purchase; and a three-keystone architecture — every SKU carries one machinery-protection keystone chosen for the biology (AstaZine® astaxanthin · Kaneka QH® ubiquinol · MitoPrime® ergothioneine), independently evidenced, with no synergy claims. concept-deck/signal_stack_architecture_framework.md · ingredient-sources/README.md

Note: exact per-SKU formulations are pre-formulation / provisional and may change. This kit names the architecture, not locked doses.

08 · Packaging & artifacts

three tiers. one trace.

The brand’s physical world is a three-tier “Halton” system: the daily packet, the monthly vessel, and the annual heirloom container — plus the Send a Signal gifting set. The world is built from artifacts, not logos: an artifact carries a sign without announcing it.

Tier 1 — the daily packet

The public-facing object. Signal Red surface, no claim, no CTA — just “send the right / sɪgnɑːl /.” The packet is the dose. Clear back, product visible.

Tier 2 — the monthly vessel

The felt-in-hands object — the latest render is the petal-fold. The vessel is the third-party proof: the brand made physical on a shelf or counter.

Tier 3 — the annual / travel container

The heirloom tier — the daily carry and the year-marker. Matte red anodized aluminum, IPA wordmark debossed; the first non-packet hardware to carry the mark. The earned annual coin is the companion recognition artifact — a real silver dollar with the IPA wordmark debossed, given after twelve unbroken months of the practice.

Send a Signal — the gifting set

Gifting, not referral. After a customer commits to their own order, they may send a $5 packet to one or two people closest to them. The recipient gets a designed artifact — envelope, card, IPA-coded message — with no incentive language, no discount code, no marketing surface. “The first signal isn’t from us. It’s from the person who already cares about them.”

09 · Imagery & visual register

the format is the canon.

The brief in one word: still. Still here. Still ready. Still doing the thing. The antithesis is wellness-and-influencer imagery — the customer is the subject of the practice, not the object of a photo.

You don’t feel a foundation. You feel everything that stands on it.

the primary format — POV, ~30 seconds of routine

The brand’s primary format is the canon, not any single film. Camera = the subject’s eyes. Approximately 30 seconds of a daily situation, first-person, body out of frame, the routine continuing. Signal lands as a single beat inside it — picked up mid-task, the chew snapping, the empty packet set down. Raw. Unstaged. Ambient.

Signal is a punctuation mark inside the routine. Not the sentence.

Other formats serve the primary, never replace it. Stills are extracts from it, framed first-person where possible. Third-person framing is allowed only when a surface needs it. Product and packaging photography live in their own register (the artifact register), shot on solid surface color — never staged into the editorial POV format.
