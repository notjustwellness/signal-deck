Signal — Unit Economics

Fundraising Stage · Prepared 2026-05-30 · Updated 2026-06-04 · INSDR Wellness Co. / Signal

Read this first. Signal is pre-launch. Formulation is underway with our contract formulator (Zone Halo). The cost figures below are a bottom-up estimate — built from named branded suppliers and anchored by three real ingredient costs (Kaneka QH® ubiquinol, MitoPrime® ergothioneine at $980/kg, creatine at $11/kg) plus a preliminary Zone Halo estimate on the daily-fuel SKU. The hero (N°001) is expressed as a validated range, not a single point. They are not yet a fully validated quote; locking the hard cost stack is the explicit first use of seed proceeds (see bottom). We'd rather show an honest, defensible cost structure than a flattering invented one.

The honest headline

Signal is a premium functional-chew brand. Chews built on branded actives are structurally more expensive to make than capsules. Earlier industry benchmarking suggested a cautious ~35–45% gross margin. An updated bottom-up estimate points comfortably above that — on the order of ~53–67% gross margin on the hero (≈60% planning case) — because real ingredient costs came in well below benchmark (the premium actives are milligram-dosed, so they cost little) and a formulation change (spermidine → NNB Puremidine™) removed the last gram-scale cost line. The single most expensive line is the Kaneka QH® ubiquinol, whose real quote sets the width of the range. We treat this as an estimate to be confirmed by the validated formulation quote, not a number to lean on yet.

Current formulation (what's being costed)

N°001 — the foundation (hero, launches first). A dual-chew daily packet. Keystone chew: Albion TRAACS® magnesium bisglycinate + AstaZine® astaxanthin + Kaneka QH® ubiquinol + MitoPrime® ergothioneine, plus sensory cofactors. Substrate chew: a phospholipid complex (membrane building blocks; delivery vehicle — in-chew vs companion softgel — set at formulation) + BioPQQ® + Puremidine™ spermidine (NNB Nutrition; ≥98% purity, ~3 mg) + gingerol.

N°002 — the daily fuel (launches 2027). Creatine + magnesium lead pair + branded ATP cofactors (betaine, ribose, methylated B-complex).

Formulation principle — minimum effective dose, maximum effect. Every active is dosed to its clinically evidenced level: enough to do real work, nothing wasted — no megadose theater, no fairy dust. This is both the brand ethos (real doses that earn their place) and the margin engine (the premium actives are milligram-dosed, so they cost little). Ingredients are not yet finalized — alternative branded actives may substitute at formulation — which is exactly why the hero cost is shown as a range, not a single point.

The honest update (2026-06-04). An updated bottom-up estimate — named branded suppliers, now three real cost anchors (Kaneka QH® ubiquinol, MitoPrime $980/kg, creatine $11/kg) — puts the foundation's product COGS in a validated range of ~$19–27 (planning case ~$22), far below the earlier $42–90 benchmark. Why the benchmark was high: the premium actives are dosed in milligrams (so they cost little), the bulky lines are cheap commodities, and switching spermidine to NNB's Puremidine™ (≥98% purity, ~3 mg vs an 800 mg wheat-germ extract) removed the last gram-scale cost. The range is driven mainly by the ubiquinol grade (its real quote spans the band) and by overage/yield not yet locked. Across that range the foundation clears ~53–67% GM at $89 (≈60% planning) — even the conservative end sits well above the original cautious 35–45% benchmark, so hero margin is not the risk it once looked like. The daily fuel (N°002) is independently estimated by Zone Halo at $10–14. Still an estimate, not a validated quote: two real anchors are in hand for the hero, six more actives need direct quotes, and locking the hard cost stack remains the first use of funds.

Margin levers — how we protect and improve GM

  1. Cost came in low. The bottom-up range (~$19–27 hero / $10–14 fuel) clears ~53–67% on the hero — the minimum-effective-dose premium actives (milligram-dosed) and the Puremidine swap are why. Validation is the lever that turns this from estimate to fact.
  2. Premium price tier — $89 is the foundation floor; positioning supports $99–120 (AG1 sits at ~$99). Pricing headroom is upside on top of the estimated margin.
  3. MOQ scale — moving from a 10K launch run toward 25K–50K pulls per-unit cost down an estimated 20–40% (Zone Halo: ~$12→$9.50 on the fuel SKU at 50K).
  4. Packaging — a fresh kraft outer + signal-red paperboard petal-fold tray each month (low-cost paperboard, no durable hardware). Per-box packaging cost is TBD pending quote and folds into the stack above.

Channels — how acquisition stays efficient

  • Practitioner channel (Fullscript) — listing at launch puts Signal in front of clinicians who already test and recommend; a recommendation transfers trust that DTC otherwise pays ~$45+/purchase to manufacture. Lowers blended CAC and is the natural home for the test-led story.
  • Send a Signal — gifting at checkout (~15% of acquisition); high-conversion, compounds the base, not the volume engine.
  • Owned DTC + organic — the brand's home; paid capped (~$35).

Repeat economics

  • Contribution / box — with the updated COGS: N°001 ~$47–61 (plan ~$54), N°002 ~$24; blended (assume 60% fuel / 40% foundation by units) ~$35–40/box (was ~$14–23 on the old benchmark COGS). The hero is only ~40% of units, so the COGS range barely moves the blended figure.
  • Retained cycles — base assumption ~6 (range bear ~4 / bull ~9), monthly auto-replenish.
  • Lifetime value (base, ~6 cycles): ~$180–220 — up from the prior $85–140, driven by the lower COGS.
  • Blended CAC target: ~$35–45 — Send-a-Signal gifting, practitioner referral, organic/founder reach, paid capped at $35.
  • LTV / CAC: ~3–5× at base on the estimated COGS (vs ~2–3× at benchmark) — and the conservative end already clears a healthy payback. Firms up as the cost estimate validates.
  • Model: subscription — monthly auto-replenish, the structure that matches the daily-practice thesis; cancel anytime, one tap.
